# 8-Week Lean Bulk Plan (Recovery-First)

## Purpose

Define an 8-week lean bulk phase with recovery-first practices (naps, sleep) to support partitioning during baby sleep disruption.

## Scope

Personal health planning; targets, recovery practices, and training structure for the block. Not a medical or coaching prescription.

---

**Created:** 2026-02-24  
**Context:** Bulk-phase paradox chat (Technogym trend Jun 2025–Jan 2026). Goal: lean into a true lean bulk while improving recovery (naps, sleep practices) to support partitioning during baby sleep disruption.

**Baseline (from thread):** ~79–80 kg, ~70 kg lean, FFMI ~21.2–21.7, ~12–13% body fat. Target end state: 82–83 kg at 10–12% (long term); this block aims to add lean mass with controlled fat gain.

---

## 1. Calorie and weight targets

| Variable | Target | Checkpoint |
|----------|--------|------------|
| Surplus | +250–300 kcal/day (above current maintenance) | Log 3–5 days/week to confirm |
| Weight gain | +0.25–0.4 kg/month (~0.5–0.8 kg over 8 weeks) | Weekly morning fasted weight |
| Waist ceiling | Increase &lt;0.5 cm/month | Weekly morning waist (same conditions) |
| Protein | ≥2 g/kg (e.g. ~160 g at 80 kg) | Daily minimum |

If weight stalls for 2 weeks and training/volume are on plan, add ~100 kcal. If waist jumps &gt;1 cm in a month, hold calories and reassess.

---

## 2. Recovery practices (naps and sleep)

Treat recovery as a non-negotiable lever so surplus supports muscle instead of fat creep.

### Naps

- **Target:** One nap daily, 15–30 min (or one 20-min nap if schedule allows only one).
- **Timing:** Early afternoon (e.g. 13:00–15:00) to avoid evening sleep pressure.
- **Practice:** Set alarm; if you don’t sleep, rest in a dark room (still reduces cortisol).
- **Checkpoint:** Note nap days per week; aim for at least 5/7.

### Sleep hygiene (baby-constrained)

- Fixed wake time ±30 min where possible.
- No screens 30–45 min before target bedtime.
- Keep bedroom cool and dark; earplugs/eye mask if helpful.
- If you’re on night duty: protect the next day’s nap and the following night (no “catch-up” chaos).

### Other recovery practices

- **Steps/activity:** Keep daily steps stable; avoid big spikes on rest days that could blunt recovery.
- **Stress:** One short block (e.g. 5–10 min breathing, walk, or stretch) on high-stress days.
- **Caffeine:** Last caffeine by a set time (e.g. 14:00–15:00) to protect evening sleep.

---

## 3. Training

- **Frequency:** Keep 3x/week; same days if that’s working.
- **Progressive overload:** Add load or reps in at least one main lift per week (e.g. +2.5 kg or +1 rep per set on 1–2 key exercises).
- **Volume:** Add 1–2 sets per week for one lagging muscle group (e.g. delts or upper back) for 2–3 weeks, then hold.
- **Near-failure:** At least one working set per exercise within 0–2 RIR on compound lifts.
- **Log:** Session summary (main lifts, RPE or RIR, any new PRs) so you can see progression.

---

## 4. Weekly and block checkpoints

### Every week (Sunday or Monday)

- [ ] Average weight (last 7 days) vs previous week.
- [ ] Waist (single measurement, same conditions).
- [ ] Nap count (days with a nap).
- [ ] Training: did you hit 3 sessions and add load/reps/sets somewhere?

### Week 4 (mid-block)

- [ ] Weight trend: ~+0.25–0.5 kg from week 0?
- [ ] Waist: &lt;1 cm total increase?
- [ ] Strength: small PRs or rep progress on main lifts?
- **Decision:** If weight flat and recovery improved, consider +100 kcal for weeks 5–8. If waist up &gt;1 cm, hold calories and keep surplus as-is.

### Week 8 (end of block)

- [ ] Weight: +0.5–0.8 kg from start?
- [ ] Waist: total increase &lt;1–1.5 cm?
- [ ] FFMI / lean mass (optional): Technogym or same scale, same conditions; look at trend, not single reading.
- **Decision:** Mini cut (6–8 weeks to ~76–77 kg, ~9–10%) or continue lean bulk for another 8 weeks with same rules.

**Surplus +250–300 kcal, +0.25–0.4 kg/month, waist &lt;0.5 cm/month; daily 15–30 min nap + fixed wake + sleep hygiene; 3x training with weekly overload and 1–2 extra sets on a lagging area; review at week 4 and 8.**
